The Black Business Professionals Association Research Analysis and Report Presented to Paul Tsaparis, The Chair of York University Board of Governors Prepared by York Communication Departmen

Introduction -

Many charitable organizations submit applications to York University each year, asking for assistance in the form of funding, resources, or partnerships. This year, The Black Business Professionals Association, the most likely contender, is presented to the York University Board of Governors by the communications department of York University. This non-profit organization seeks to offer initiatives that support economic growth, higher education, and corporate and professional excellence. They also seek to advance Black communities in Canada. Their objective is to become the go-to organization for the business, financial, and industry development needs of Black people. The ambition of York University, which is to pursue, conserve, and share knowledge, is the primary measure used to evaluate the charity. In the domains of science, technology, engineering, and medicine, York promises to excel in research and teaching. York develops critical thinking while testing the limits and frameworks of knowledge. They strive for excellence in carrying out all parts of its purpose through its vision and core principles, which include providing a wide range of students with access to a high-calibre, research-intensive learning environment dedicated to the public good. They support sustainability on all fronts—environmental, social, and economic—as a crucial compass for choices and actions. York University is the ideal long-term sponsor for The Black Business Professionals Association since York is already a community of teachers, students, staff, alumni, and volunteers dedicated to academic freedom, social justice, accessible education, and collegial self-governance.

Discussion -

The non-profit charity organization, The Black Business Professionals Association, was established in 1983 to address issues of equity and opportunity for the Black community in business, employment, education, and economic development (About The Black Business and Professionals Association, 2022)

Goals -

The mission of the BBPA is to support higher education, economic development, and business and professional excellence to advance Black communities in Canada. The vision of BBPA is to become the go-to organization for the business, professional, and economic development requirements of the Black community. The purpose of BBPA is to promote and aid in the pursuit of business, professional excellence, higher learning, and economic empowerment. They continuously work to make it easier to access people and services. They recognize and honour exceptional performance. The BBPA creates connections and collaborates with other organizations on issues involving commerce, education, economic growth, and neighbourhood well-being. They foster equity and cross-cultural understanding

The BBPA National Scholarship Program (NSP)-

The BBPA continues to achieve its goals through several programs. One of these initiatives is the BBPA National Scholarship Program (NSP), which was started in 1986 to encourage and assist Black Canadian youth in attaining academic excellence through an annual scholarship program. It was developed in response to the need to lower the rate at which Black pupils drop out of school owing to financial hardship, despite having rising intellectual functioning. The NSP is run independently from the Harry Jerome Awards and all other BBPA activities by a voluntary board of trustees appointed by the Black Business and Professional Association (BBPA). The NSP is supported by mentorship programs, HR programs, financial literacy programs, skills training, and more to give applicants a firmer foundation. The NSP receives financial support from businesses and people who share its objective to encourage the education and leadership development of Black youths in Canada by aiding them in pursuing post-secondary education. Donors have the option of making contributions to the General Fund, which helps the charity with operating expenses and is used to fund scholarships, or to the Endowment Fund, which was set up to provide a stable financial foundation for the growth of the annual scholarships and to establish a scholarship program that can be ongoingly be maintained

Scholarships-

he BBPA Skilled Trades Scholarship receives funding each year and was initially made available in 2020. This scholarship honours exceptional student who wants to pursue their preferred skilled trade. The BBPA is aware of the need to close the generational gap between business and our youth and to advance the economic system at the individual, corporate, and governmental levels. Therefore, it is given to a graduate of high school who has demonstrated academic achievement, social responsibility, and personal needs. BBPA Named or Legacy Scholarships are directly supported by corporations or Black Canadians who have contributed to and are still contributing to the enrichment of Canada in the fields of the arts, 7 professions, science, commerce, technology, politics, and other endeavours. This has been created in honour of the chosen person or named after the chosen corporation. It is typically given to exceptional kids who have demonstrated academic excellence, volunteerism, or with special needs. The BBPA distributed 54 scholarships worth close to $180,000 in 2019. The BBPA Scholarship Fund has awarded over 1,150 scholarships totalling $4.5 million over the last 34 years. (Deveau, Lajartre, & Craig-Bourdin, 2022). The goal of the BBPA National Scholarship Fund is to assist Black kids in achieving their goals! The annual BBPA Harry Jerome Awards, a national awards ceremony that honours and rewards distinction in achievement, are produced by the BBPA. The BBPA National Scholarship helps students pursue further education. The National Black Business and Professional Conference is another event sponsored by the BBPA, and they collaborate with them to support community activities.

Scholarships-

Donations and special event revenues for the BBPA in 2021 total $2.3 million. Less investment income, administrative expenses are 4% of revenues, and fundraising expenses are 28% of gifts and special events. This has led to an increase of 8% since 2020 in overall overhead spending to 32%. In addition, Starbucks, Bell Canada, RBC, and many more give money to the BBPA (BBPA Financial Statement, 2022)

Business Advisory Implementation Development Services (BAIDS) Program-

An intriguing event is the charity's "Business Advisory Implementation Development Services (BAIDS) Program." The initiative aims to strengthen the Black business community in Canada after the destruction caused by the pandemic. The BBPA travelled to St. John's, Fredericton, Montreal, Thunder Bay, Vancouver, and someday York University to interact with and assist Black-led businesses across Canada. As a result, BAIDS was created to give Black company owners the tools and knowledge they need to thrive in Canada's cutthroat business environment. It is a tailored program to empower Black-owned enterprises lacking the necessary tools and knowledge to do crucial work-related tasks. "The cost of managing impressions for Black employees: Perspective from the expectancy violation theory," written by Wayne, reveals the specific bias that Black employees experience, making it challenging for this group to manage their professional image. The article discusses the need for institutions like York University to comprehend the reality of the Black experience, which calls for us to support initiatives like the BBPA that help level the playing field. It will be difficult and occasionally uncomfortable work. However, it is worthwhile for the Black community and the many other oppressed or underrepresented groups. Organizations and society should work harder than ever to use everyone, not just a select few, people's experiences, expertise, and abilities. Corporate executives may make a considerably more significant and immediate difference. Senator Obama at the time remarked in 2008, "Change will not come if we wait for some other person or if we wait for some other time. We are the ones we have been waiting for for. We are the change that we seek." (Wayne, Kluemper, Cheung, & Ubaka, 2022)

Conclusion -

The Black Business Professionals Association has spent the last 38 years supporting Black business communities, youth, and professionals to ensure no one is left behind. The BBPA keeps up with that pledge through numerous programs. York is a progressive community that values open-minded inquiry, fresh ideas, and futuristic solutions. As mentioned earlier, the Black Business Professionals Association is an ideal candidate for York's sponsorship due to the reciprocal benefits resulting from the explanation above. The alliance would increase York's visibility in the black community, inspire donations and other events at York, strengthen ties with the Black community, and encourage giving to important causes. York is dedicated to improving social justice and equity through critical analysis, original thinking, and civic engagement. They promote inclusivity and diversity, welcoming other viewpoints, cultures, and learning modes while encouraging global fluency and cross-cultural understanding. There is no better corporation for the BBPA to refer to as a sponsor than York University
