Stephen Lecce
The Persistent Politician of Ontario

By: Kenzie Billard

Lecce was voted in as Ontario's Minister of Education on June 20, 2019. Premier Doug Ford proclaimed a state of emergency on March 17, 2020, due to COVID-19, and Lecce introduced Bill 28, also known as the Keeping Students in Class Act, which was adopted on November 3, 2022, by the Legislative Assembly of Ontario

Ontario will launch consultations on reevaluating some skilled trades' grade 12 education requirements this year. According to Lecce, the Ford administration wants every student to have a path to a job, home ownership, and a life of fulfillment and purpose. Lecce supports an open educational system because he feels maximizing the potential of our children will be crucial to our future.

Lecce’s first political involvement was during the 1999 election of the late MPP Al Palladini in his hometown of Vaughan. Since then, he has worked as former chief spokesperson, Deputy Government House Leader, the Parliamentary Assistant to the Ministry of Infrastructure, and the Parliamentary Assistant to the Premier. As an MPP, a former NCFirst Vice President of the National Congress of Italian Canadians, an active member of the Vaughan Chamber of Commerce, and a former Youth Education Chair of the Vaughan Royal Canadian Legion, he continues to support and give back to the residents of King-Vaughan.

"I am an outspoken supporter of public education and a proud graduate of St. Margaret Mary Catholic Elementary in Vaughan. I also have nieces who attend public schools," says Lecce. "I'm 33 and use a generational lens to examine how public policy affects young people, which historically has not been the standard in the cabinet."

As I interviewed Lecce more than three weeks ago, I asked him if he saw the irony that the province was still experiencing a horrible education crisis even in the absence of this horrific pandemic. When Lecce replied that he had just phoned all the union presidents personally — twice, in fact, that week — I think I caught a glimpse of a smile. He recalled telling them that "lives are on the line, and it calls for all of us to put differences aside and work together for the kids and the employees. So I appreciate our working together as a team."

Despite his strong views, Lecce is a politician who listens to others. He incorporates their opinions into his decision-making process, making him an effective leader. The community and Lecce's colleagues appreciate his dedication to the betterment of Ontario's education system. However, his approach has also met with criticism, particularly during the pandemic.

Critics argue that his policies on education during the pandemic are insufficient and that Ontario's schools have been hit hard by the pandemic. They believe that students and educators need more support and resources and that Lecce's approach needs to provide those resources. However, Lecce disagrees and believes he is doing everything he can to ensure students and educators have what they need to succeed.

As Jack Fazzari, Lecce's assistant, and I spoke about the Minister's role in the educational system, Fazzari mentioned that there is no substitute for in-class instruction, which is why they worked diligently to get students back to school in a safe and responsible way. He stated, "Our priority has always been the safety and well-being of students and staff. Minister Lecce has been working tirelessly with health officials and education partners to ensure schools remain safe and open."

Lecce's dedication to education reform and advocacy for student success has been noticed. He has been recognized by numerous organizations, including the Canada India Foundation and the Canadian Italian Business and Professional Association, for his contributions to public service and his leadership in promoting diversity and inclusivity.

Despite the challenges he has faced and the criticism he has received, Lecce remains committed to his vision for the future of education in Ontario. He believes that every student deserves the opportunity to succeed and that it is the government's responsibility to provide the resources and support necessary to make that happen.

In conclusion, Stephen Lecce is a persistent politician who is passionate about education reform and advocating for student success. Through his work as Ontario's Minister of Education, he has implemented policies aimed at ensuring that every student has the opportunity to acquire the transferable skills necessary to lead fulfilling lives and satisfying employment. Despite facing criticism and challenges along the way, Lecce remains committed to his vision and is determined to create a better future for Ontario's students.
